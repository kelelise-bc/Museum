Meet the Team

Dr. Elara Venn - The Memory Chaser

Subject Matter Expertise: Analog Archaeologist / Memory Excavator

Professional Bio

Dr. Elara Venn is an archaeologist who specializes in the recovery and preservation of photographic artifacts — negatives, undeveloped rolls, and decayed prints. With advanced training in analog restoration and digital imaging at the University of Edinburgh, she blends chemistry, art history, and data preservation. Her work bridges the tangible and the ephemeral, exploring how light, once captured, continues to speak. Calm, methodical, and quietly poetic, she believes every frame she rescues is an act of resurrection.

Museum Role

Leads The Excavation Lab at the Museum of Forgotten Photographs. Oversees all artifact recovery operations, scanning protocols, and preservation documentation for newly unearthed photographic materials.

Special Considerations

Recipient of the Lumen Foundation Fellowship for her research on “Chemical Memory in Damaged Negatives.” She is partially color-blind, which she considers a gift — allowing her to perceive tone, shape, and shadow as pure form rather than distraction.

Dr. Rhea Anwar

Subject Matter Expertise: Visual Anthropologist / Human Trace Analyst

Professional Bio

Dr. Anwar earned her Ph.D. in Cultural Anthropology from the University of Toronto, focusing on visual ethnography and the semiotics of family photography. She studies the social and emotional residue embedded in forgotten photographs, decoding posture, gesture, and spatial tension. Rhea’s methods combine academic rigor with intuitive empathy, resulting in field reports that read like both research and elegy. She is fluent in four languages and trained in handwriting and symbol analysis.

Museum Role

Conducts the Human Context Analysis for the museum’s recovered photographic collections, reconstructing social and emotional narratives from anonymous subjects. Works closely with the Interpreter to integrate her findings into exhibitions.

Special Considerations

Born in Karachi and raised between Pakistan and Canada, she brings a diasporic lens to her work, often exploring how displacement shapes the photographic record. Known for her deep listening skills and her insistence that “silence is also data.”

Prof. Iole Voss

Interpretive Discipline: Symbolic Archivist / Theoretical Ethnographer

Professional Bio

Professor Iole Voss is a cultural historian and semiotician trained at the Sorbonne and the University of Copenhagen. His research centers on how societies build mythologies from images — what he calls “photographic folklore.” He interprets recovered artifacts through the lens of narrative theory, drawing connections between visual silence and collective memory. Analytical yet lyrical, He sees each photograph as a ceremony of remembrance.

Interpretive Method

Combines semiotic analysis, AI-assisted narrative generation, and mythographic comparison to derive meaning from recovered photographs. Collaborates with anthropologists to ensure interpretations remain grounded while allowing for symbolic resonance. His essays form the foundation of the museum’s Histories section.

Special Considerations

Advocates for a post-colonial approach to interpretation, emphasizing ethical storytelling and respect for the unknowable. Known for challenging the boundaries between research and ritual, he often opens his lectures with the phrase: “Every photograph is an unfinished sentence — I listen for the words that time forgot.”
