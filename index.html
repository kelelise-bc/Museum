Bringing Light to Forgotten Stories

Step inside a museum dedicated to the unseen and the unremembered — where lost negatives, forgotten rolls of film, and unlabeled prints are given new life. Each recovered image reveals fragments of time once thought to have vanished.

We collect, restore, and recontextualize the photographs left behind — honoring the unknown photographers whose vision was nearly erased by history.

Join us as we piece together stories from light, shadow, and silence. Volunteer to help with digitization, research, and exhibition preparation.

Programs and Events

Grand Opening Exhibition

Reflections of the Past: The Colours of Time and Memory

Explore rediscovered archives spanning a century of lost photography — from unclaimed film rolls to attic negatives restored through digital and chemical processes.

Exhibits include:

  • The Maier Collection: Street life recovered from undeveloped film
  • Found in Dust: Images salvaged from estate sales and forgotten basements
  • Echo Portraits: Anonymous faces matched to letters, postcards, and fragments of identity

Seasonal Series: Shadows Reimagined

Quarterly exhibitions exploring different themes of memory and loss in photography.

  • Winter 2025: The Absent Frame — what’s left unseen beyond the edge of the photo
  • Spring 2026: Ghost Light — the play of time, exposure, and fading memory
  • Summer 2026: Afterimage — rediscovered Kodachrome, 1940–1980

Community and Research Center

A space for collaboration between photographers, historians, and the public.

  • Workshops: Film preservation, scanning, restoration, and metadata cataloging
  • Talks: Conversations with archivists and visual anthropologists
  • Projects: Help identify anonymous photographers through open-source crowdsourcing
